Annotation Guidelines
Right-Aligned Narratives (Farmer’s Protest)
The narratives aligned with the right during the Farmers' Protest primarily seek to legitimize the policies of the central government and delegitimize the protests by framing them as politically motivated, externally influenced, or misinformed movements. These narratives emphasize reform, national stability, and patriotic duty while portraying dissenting voices as disruptive or misguided.
Glorification of the Central Government
 Articles portraying the central government as visionary, reformist, or acting in farmers’ best interest.
 Cues:


Presents farm laws as historic, bold, or progressive.


Credits leadership for modernization or economic reform.


Highlights efficiency, decisiveness, or long-term vision.


Example: “The PM's reform vision will finally free farmers from middlemen.” / “These laws mark a new era of prosperity for rural India.”


Justifying Farm Laws by Critiquing Existing Policies
 Articles defending the new farm laws by emphasizing flaws or inefficiencies of the older system.
 Cues:


Frames previous market structures as exploitative or outdated.


Argues that reforms are necessary corrections.


Example: “For decades, farmers were trapped in the APMC monopoly.” / “The old system benefited brokers, not farmers.”


Vilification of the Opposition
 Articles blaming opposition parties or leaders for politicizing or misleading farmers.
 Cues:


Claims protests are driven by opposition propaganda.


Describes leaders as hypocritical or self-serving.


Example: “Opposition leaders are inciting unrest for political mileage.” / “Those who failed farmers for 60 years now pretend to care.”


Framing Anti-Farm Law Protests as Subversive
 Articles depicting the protests as anti-national or disruptive to peace.
 Cues:


References funding or conspiracy.


Describes protests as violent, separatist, or extremist.


Example: “These so-called protests are an attempt to destabilize the nation.”


Criticizing Global Figures and Celebrities
 Articles discrediting international figures or celebrities who expressed solidarity with farmers.
 Cues:


Dismisses foreign commentary as ignorant or biased.


Emphasizes domestic sovereignty over external opinions.


Example: “Foreign celebrities should stop lecturing India without understanding facts.” / “These global influencers are tools of anti-India campaigns.”
Left-Aligned Narratives (Farmer’s Protest)
The narratives aligned with the left during the Farmers' Protest primarily seek to delegitimize the central government and legitimize the protesters by framing them as democratic, oppressed, or morally justified. These narratives emphasize injustice, people’s resistance, and human rights while criticizing state repression and propaganda.
Vilification of the Central Government
 Articles accusing the government of arrogance, repression, or lack of empathy toward farmers.
 Cues:


Frames the government as authoritarian or indifferent.


Uses moral condemnation or emotional tone.


Example: “The government silences farmers instead of hearing their pain.” / “Farmers' pleas are ignored by those in power.”


Depicting Farmers as Victims
 Articles portray farmers as suffering under unjust laws or state apathy.
 Cues:


Focuses on the hardships, deaths, or sacrifices of protesters.


Evokes empathy and moral outrage.


Example: “Elderly farmers spend nights on the streets for their rights.” / “Protesters continue to die, but their voices remain unheard.”


Emphasizing Global and Celebrity Endorsements
 Articles highlighting international attention or celebrity support for protests to validate legitimacy.
 Cues:


Cites solidarity tweets, global rallies, or NGO statements.


Frames global support as moral validation.


Example: “Global icons from across continents have supported India’s farmers.” / “The world is watching as India’s farmers fight for justice.”


Accusing Media and Government of Manipulation
 Articles claiming the media and government misrepresent or suppress protest realities.
 Cues:


Mentions biased reporting or censorship.


Suggests narrative control or propaganda.


Example: “The media is running government-fed lies about farmers’ violence.” / “Independent journalists face harassment for showing ground truth.”


Right-Aligned Narratives (CAA/NRC)
The narratives aligned with the right during the CAA/NRC debates primarily seek to legitimize the policies of the central government and delegitimize the protests by framing them as politically motivated, externally influenced, or misinformed. These narratives emphasize reform, national stability, and patriotic duty while portraying dissenting voices as disruptive or misguided.
Glorification of CAA
 Articles portraying the Citizenship Amendment Act as patriotic, humanitarian, or constitutionally sound.
 Cues:


Emphasizes moral duty or compassion.


Links CAA to India's civilizational ethos.


Example: “CAA offers refuge to persecuted minorities—it’s an act of compassion.” / “This law strengthens India’s humanitarian spirit.”


Glorification of the Central Government
 Articles presenting the government as the protector of India’s sovereignty or integrity.
 Cues:


Frames CAA/NRC as a defense against illegal immigration.


Appeals to national unity or security.


Example: “The government stands firm to protect India’s borders and identity.”


Vilification of the Opposition
 Articles that morally discredit or delegitimize opposition parties, portraying them as politically motivated, anti-national, or obstructive to government initiatives.
 Cues:


Frames the opposition as divisive or acting against national interest.


Uses morally charged language to question credibility or intent.


Example: “Opposition leaders are opposing CAA only to appease vote banks.” / “They are obstructing reforms meant for the welfare of persecuted minorities.”


Opposition Spreading Misinformation and Fear
 Articles that cognitively discredit the opposition or media by accusing them of spreading falsehoods, misinterpretations, or panic regarding the CAA/NRC.
 Cues:


Mentions deliberate fear-mongering or fake news campaigns.


Highlights ignorance or distortion of the law's provisions.


Frames unrest as a result of misinformation rather than a legitimate grievance.


Example: “False propaganda is misleading citizens about CAA.” / “Unfounded rumors are fueling unnecessary fear among minorities.”


Anti-CAA Protests as Pre-Planned and Funded
 Articles alleging that protests are orchestrated or foreign-funded to defame India.
 Cues:


Mentions NGOs, conspiracies, or hidden motives.


Frames unrest as part of an international agenda.


Example: “The protests were planned months before the Act was passed.” / “Foreign agencies are behind the chaos.”


Framing Anti-CAA Protests as Subversive or Misguided
 Articles portray protesters as misinformed, anti-national, or extremist.
 Cues:


Frames protests as threats to national unity.


Suggests participants are misled or ignorant.


Example: “Protesters have no idea what the law even says.”


Left-Aligned Narratives (CAA/NRC)
The narratives aligned with the left during the CAA/NRC debates primarily seek to delegitimize the central government and legitimize the protesters by framing them as defenders of democracy and secularism. These narratives emphasize moral injustice, civil resistance, and government overreach.
Vilification of CAA
 Articles framing the law as discriminatory, unconstitutional, or anti-minority.
 Cues:


Highlights the exclusion of Muslims or the erosion of secularism.


Uses moral or legal condemnation.


Example: “CAA legitimizes religious discrimination under the guise of compassion.” / “This Act dismantles India’s secular foundation.”


Vilification of the Central Government
 Articles accusing the government of authoritarianism, repression, or communal politics.
 Cues:


Focuses on police brutality or suppression of dissent.


Frames leadership as divisive or intolerant.


Example: “The government treats peaceful protesters as enemies.” / “This is an attack on dissent, not governance.”


Delegitimization of Critics
 Articles suggesting critics of protests or secularism are biased or communal.
 Cues:


Attacks right-wing commentators or state-aligned voices.


Calls out intolerance disguised as patriotism.


Example: “Those defending CAA expose their deep-rooted prejudice.” / “Supporters of the Act peddle hate in the name of patriotism.”


Glorifying Anti-CAA Protesters
 Articles portraying protesters as brave defenders of democracy and justice.
 Cues:


Describes protests as peaceful or youth-led.


Frames protesters as voices of conscience.


Example: “Women of Shaheen Bagh became the conscience of the nation.” / “Students raised their voices to protect India’s soul.”


Framing Anti-CAA Protesters as Victims
 Articles emphasizing state violence or persecution against protesters.
 Cues:


Focuses on casualties, injuries, or arrests.


Highlights moral injustice or fear.


Example: “Protesters face jail for exercising free speech.”
